Offers (by company)

Sakura Mobile

They have two wifi offers:

  • First one run on the SoftBank 4G/LTE network, with data caps of 5GB, 30GB or 100GB/month.
  • Second one from UQ WiMax, which is “unlimited” (but if you exceed 10GB over three days, you will be throttled).

The minimum contract period is three months, and there is a 5500 yen activation fee for both offers.

Cost per plan:

  • 5GB plan is 3828 yen/month.
  • 30GB plan is 5478 yen/month.
  • 100GB plan is 7128 yen/month.

Features:

  • Cancellation: Free
  • Free contract suspension: YES
  • English support: YES

Rakuten Mobile Wifi

Rakuten is trying to attract customers with an unlimited data offer. Its principle is that "the amount of data you use each month determines what you pay".

Cost:

  • Less than 1GB = free
  • From 1GB to 3GB = 1078 yen
  • From 3GB to 20GB = 2178 yen
  • More than 20GB = 3278 yen

Features:

  • No activation fee
  • Speed may differ significantly depending on your area.

UQ mobile

UQ mobile has three phone plans:

  • S (1628 yen/3GB monthly)
  • M (2728 yen/15GB monthly)
  • L (3828 yen/25GB monthly)

Feature

  • The ability to rollover remaining data to the following month. This way, you can use all of your data without any waste.
  • Domestic calls are 22 yen per 30 seconds. You can also add an unlimited calling plan as an option.

IIJmio(miofone)

You can choose a plan for your features(data only, call and data, SMS, etc.) and data amount (2GB, 4GB, 8GB, 15GB, 20GB).

  • The most expensive has call and data capabilities at 2068 yen for 20GB of data.
  • The cheapest plan for both call and data features is 858 yen at 2GB.

GTN MOBILE

GTN Mobile is a telephone service provider for foreign residents with SIM cards in Japan. With GTN MOBILE, it easy to communicate and access the Internet on your mobile phone. They have a selection of inexpensive plans with charges starting at 2178 yen for 30GB of data.

The GTN MOBILE website is available in seven languages: Japanese, English, Chinese, Korean, Vietnamese, Nepali and Indonesian. So, you can subscribe to a telephone contract even if you don't speak or read Japanese.

Note

When entering into a mobile phone contract, you should pay two types of fees:

  • Monthly fees (detailed above for each company)
  • Initial fees range from about 3000 to 5000 yen. Depending on the carrier, it may be called a registration fee, administrative fee, or activation fee. This one-time fee is payable at the time of application.
