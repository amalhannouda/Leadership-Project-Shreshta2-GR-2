Internet Services

Internet is an indispensable part of our lives and especially for students. To be up to date and follow your courses regularly, subscribing to a package with an Internet service provider is one of the first things to do. However, many people may put off signing contracts due to complicated procedures and language barriers. In Japan, there are two types of Internet connections available: wired (fixed line) and wireless.

Wired Network Connections: Fiber-Optic Hikari

Wired network connections are installed in residences, and, in general, provide high speed and high-capacity data transmission. They can be also used as Wi-Fi, but cannot be used outside of your residence. it include fiber-optic (光 or "hikari") and ADSL lines.

A fiber-optic line is the current fixed-line Internet in Japan. Fiber-option has a fast network connection and supports large amounts of data transmission. In the past, fixed-line data services used ADSL, an inexpensive but slow form of Internet. ADSL are no longer being accepted, will end completely by January 2023.

Wireless

Wireless Internet is convenient to portable WiFi devices, it allows users to connect outside but it's more expensive. it help to connect at Internet—both indoors and outdoors—via a mobile router. This one is a good choice for those staying in Japan for less than one year.
